# Community Talk

> A talk show exploring regenerative communities — guests, stories, and ideas shaping the future of neighborhood life.

---

## The Show Concept

**Format:** Long-form conversation (45–90 min) — part interview, part storytelling, part workshop
**Vibe:** Warm, curious, grounded — like a conversation with someone who's actually *doing* the work
**Audience:** People starting or growing regenerative communities, urban planners, social entrepreneurs, land trust advocates, local governance nerds

**Core Question We Always Return To:**
*How do we build neighborhoods where both people and land thrive?*

---

## Episode Format

Each episode follows a loose structure:

1. **Opening (5 min)** — The guest's "origin story" — how they got into this work
2. **Deep Dive (30–50 min)** — One main topic per episode (see below)
3. **Rapid Fire (10 min)** — 5 quick questions: favorite community, most underrated tool, daily practice, etc.
4. **Close (5 min)** — What they're building next, where to find them

---

## Season 1 Episode Topics

### Episode 1: Finding Your People
*The hardest first step — how do you actually find the humans you want to build with?*

- How did you know your community was the *right* community?
- What do you wish you'd done differently in the search?
- At what point does "finding people" become "attracting the right people"?
- Stories of communities that started with strangers and became family
- What role does AI matching play (or not play) in this?
- Quick: best place to start looking?

### Episode 2: The Land Question
*Regenerative communities need land. Who owns it, who cares for it, and how do you get access?*

- Land trust vs. cooperative ownership vs. private — what's the real difference in practice?
- Urban vs. rural — does location change everything?
- Stories from someone who actually found land: how long did it take, what did they learn?
- The economics no one talks about honestly
- How do you start if you don't have capital?
- Quick: most surprising thing about your land situation?

### Episode 3: Governance That Doesn't Suck
*Most communities have a governance problem. Here's how to actually make decisions together.*

- Why do so many intentional communities fail at governance?
- Democracy, sociocracy, meritocracy — when does each actually make sense?
- Stories: what does AI-facilitated governance actually look like in practice?
- How do you handle the person who always blocks consensus?
- The one rule that changed everything for your community
- Quick: governance tool you actually use every day?

### Episode 4: Money That Makes Sense
*Community economics without losing your soul — sustainable revenue, fair contributions, transparent finances.*

- How does a young community generate income without selling out?
- The co-op model — real numbers, real stories
- What does "fair contribution" actually mean when members have very different incomes?
- How do you handle someone who can't pay?
- Revenue diversification — what actually works vs. what sounds good
- Quick: most creative community revenue stream you've heard of?

### Episode 5: The Technology Question
*Does your community need an app? A platform? Or just a WhatsApp group and a whiteboard?*

- When does technology help and when does it create new problems?
- The case for and against AI in community coordination
- What would the "perfect" community tech stack look like?
- Stories of tech that failed and why
- Privacy and data — what should communities never share?
- Quick: one piece of tech your community couldn't live without?

### Episode 6: Conflict as Fuel
*Conflict is inevitable. The communities that survive it aren't the ones that avoid it — they're the ones that have systems for it.*

- The most important conflict resolution skill no one teaches
- What regenerative conflict looks like vs. destructive conflict
- Stories: a time conflict went really badly and what you learned
- When is leaving the right answer?
- Building psychological safety in a new community
- Quick: one book or practice that changed how you handle conflict?

### Episode 7: Growing Without Losing Your Soul
*Communities that scale often lose what made them special. How do you grow without losing the magic?*

- The "founder's curse" — when the creator becomes the bottleneck
- How do you onboard new members without diluting the culture?
- The 50-person threshold — what changes and how do you prepare?
- Stories: communities that grew and communities that chose to stay small
- When is "staying small" actually the smarter choice?
- Quick: ideal community size for your context and why?

### Episode 8: The Next Generation
*What does it take to raise kids in community? And what do young people actually want from neighborhood life?*

- Children in community — the rewards and the challenges no one prepares you for
- What are young people actually asking for from neighborhoods?
- Education: school, unschool, community school — what works?
- The intergenerational question — how do you design for both elders and youth?
- What do you wish your community had when you were growing up?
- Quick: favorite memory of community as a kid?

### Episode 9: Rituals and Culture
*The invisible glue — how communities create belonging through ceremony, rhythm, and shared stories.*

- The difference between "living together" and "being a community"
- Daily, weekly, seasonal rhythms that actually stick
- Origin stories — why the founding myth matters
- Food, fire, song — the simplest rituals that transform group dynamics
- What happens when culture becomes强迫 (forced)?
- Quick: one ritual your community does that you'd never give up?

### Episode 10: The Network Effect
*One community is a start. What happens when regenerative neighborhoods start talking to each other?*

- The Hive concept — what does an inter-community network actually do?
- Mutual aid between communities — real examples
- How do different communities stay aligned on values while maintaining autonomy?
- The challenge of "tribalism" between communities with shared missions
- What could a global network of regenerative neighborhoods actually accomplish?
- Quick: one community you'd love to visit and why?

## Guest Types to Invite

1. **Founders** — People who started communities and are willing to tell the real story
2. **Researchers** — Academics studying community design, social cohesion, ecological governance
3. **Practitioners** — Land trust managers, cooperative developers, governance consultants
4. **Dreamers** — People in the early stages building something worth watching
5. **Skeptics** — People who tried community and left — the most important stories
